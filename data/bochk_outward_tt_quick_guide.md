# Outward Remittance Quick Reference Guide

## Page 1

Outward Remittance Quick Reference Guide

Submission via an Internet banking platform is your first choice. If it is
necessary to submit via branches, use of the smart PDF Remittance Application
Form provided by BOCHK is preferred.

Fill in the details of remittance generally in English on the application.
Chinese characters are converted to telegram code during transmission and thus
it may take longer time for the Beneficiary's Bank to process or may not be
accepted.

If the beneficiary account is opened with a branch or sub-branch in the
mainland, Macau, Taiwan and certain branches or subsidiaries within the Bank of
China group outside Hong Kong, customers may use BOC Remittance Plus for more
efficient transfer of funds.

Supplying the SWIFT BIC and relevant clearing code of Beneficiary's Bank enables
automated processing and can accelerate the time of completion.

Providing details that match the records of Beneficiary's Bank on the payment
instruction can enhance efficiency and eliminate extra confirmation.

BOC Remittance Plus is a dual-direction remittance service jointly offered by
BOCHK and Bank of China. The proceeds of BOC Remittance Plus may reach the
receiving branch as fast as 5 minutes through BOCHK's highly automated process,
saving transit time for effecting payment.

BOCHK provides payment services to corporate customers through FPS.

### Cutoff Time and Same-Day Processing

The guide lists cutoff times for outward remittance channels and currencies.

For Telegraphic Transfer:

- HKD / USD / EUR / CAD / GBP: branch cutoff time 17:00; internet banking cutoff
  time 18:00.
- AUD / BND / CHF / DKK / JPY / NOK / NZD / SEK / ZAR: branch cutoff time
  17:00; internet banking cutoff time 18:00.
- Non Major Currencies: branch cutoff time 17:00; corporate internet banking
  cutoff time 18:00.

For BOC Remittance Plus:

- HKD / USD: branch cutoff time 16:00; internet banking cutoff time 16:30.
- CNY: corporate internet banking cutoff time 15:30.

Important notes:

- The above cutoff time is not applicable to Public Holidays in Hong Kong.
- BOCHK will process the remittance application received before the respective
  cutoff time within the same day, provided that:
  - the instructions are clear, complete and correct;
  - it is a business day or clearing day in the country or region of the
    Beneficiary Bank and the remittance currency;
  - source of fund is sufficient to effect the payment and pay the charges;
  - currency exchange, if applicable, is arranged for same day value.
- After the above cutoff time, adding and update of templates, scheduled
  remittance, and standing instructions can be submitted through internet
  banking platforms. Customers should refer to the service hours of the
  respective internet banking platforms.
- Unless otherwise arranged, remittance applications submitted to any branch
  after the above cutoff time will normally be processed on next business day.
- Remittance applications submitted during business hours on Saturdays,
  excluding Public Holidays in Hong Kong, will be processed on next business
  day.

### Details of Beneficiary's Bank

Customers should provide the name and address of the Beneficiary's Bank. On
Express Transfer (RTGS/CHATS) and FPS, customers should provide the 3 digit Bank
Code for local interbank transfers.

Clearing codes are used among banks and institutions to identify each other
during telecommunication and payment clearing. Providing the relevant clearing
code for party identification will speed up the processing of a remittance
transaction.

Telegraphic Transfer generally uses SWIFT BIC of 8 or 11 characters.

### Details of Beneficiary

To ensure accurate credit of funds, customers should provide correct details of
the Beneficiary Account on the instructions, including Beneficiary's Account
Number or International Bank Account Number (IBAN), and Beneficiary's Name.

For FPS transfer instructions, customers should provide Beneficiary's Account
Number, Beneficiary's Mobile Phone Number, Beneficiary's Email Address, HKID
Number or Beneficiary's FPS Identifier.

The guide also lists requirements for specific countries and regions, including
Canada, Australia, Brunei Darussalam, Canada, Egypt, Jamaica, Mongolia, the
Philippines, South Africa, Yemen, Singapore, the mainland, South Africa, and
European Economic Area / European Union remittances.

### Renminbi Remittance

Cross-border remittances to or from the mainland or other places outside Hong
Kong are subject to the rules and requirements of the jurisdiction of the
originating end and the receiving end.

For personal customer remittance to the mainland, Renminbi remittance to the
mainland is only available to Hong Kong residents, under the condition that the
Renminbi funds are remitted to the remitter's own account, meaning the same name
personal account on the mainland.

The upper limit of the daily aggregate amount remitted to the mainland per
person is CNY 80,000.

For corporate customer or company account remittance to the mainland, remittance
is allowed for settlement of trade, including goods and services, and other
purposes as allowed by the mainland authorities. Corporate customers are required
to indicate the purpose of payment on each Cross-Border Renminbi Remittance by
standard codeword on the instruction. If no indication of purpose is given, the
remittance may be delayed or rejected.

### Remittance in Non-Major Currency

BOCHK can provide outward remittance service in 100 non-major currencies such as
New Taiwan Dollar, Indian Rupee and South Korean Won.

Applications for remittance in non-major currencies can be submitted via
branches or corporate internet banking service. Cutoff time for branches same
day processing is 17:00 from Monday to Friday. Cutoff time for corporate
internet banking for same day processing is 18:00 from Monday to Friday,
excluding public holidays in Hong Kong and U.S. banking holiday.

### Charge Options

The Bank's own charges can be paid by the customer or deducted from the
remittance proceeds. For remittance of non-major currencies, deduction of the
handling charges from remittance proceeds is not accepted.

Correspondent Bank Charges mean the charges imposed by related correspondent
banks or institutions, intermediary banks or institutions, clearing banks or
institutions and/or the beneficiary bank or institution in processing the
remittance, plus BOCHK extra handling charges.
